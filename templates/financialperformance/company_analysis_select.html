Liquidity RatioA ratio that indicates a company’s ability to meet its obligations or pay its short-term debt.

Cash RatioCash Ratio is used to compare the total cash and cash equivalents of the company with its current liabilities.
.
Quick Acid RatioThe ratio used to measure a company’s ability to meet its short-term debt is soon due by using its current assets (cash, short-term securities and receivables).
.
Current RatioThe ratio compares the current assets of a company with short-term debt. Short-term debt includes trade payables, notes payable, bank debt, salary debt, and other debts that must be paid immediately (Sutrisno, 2012).
.

Solvability RatioThis ratio is often also called the leverage ratio, which is the ratio used to measure the extent to which a company’s assets are financed by its debt (Hery, 2016).

Debt to AssetDebt to asset measures how much the company’s assets are financed by its total debt. The higher this ratio, the greater of the amount of loan capital used to invest in assets, in order to generate profits for the company (Syamsuddin, 2006).
.
Debt to EquityThis ratio is used to determine the amount of funds provided by the borrower (creditor) with the owner of the company. In other words, this ratio serves to know each dollar of its own capital used for debt guarantees (Kasmir, 2014).
.
Long Term Debt to EquityThis ratio is used to measure the proportion of long-term debt to capital or equity. In other words, this ratio is used to measure what part of each Rupiah of capital or equity is used as a guarantee of long-term debt (Hery, 2016).
.
Coverage RatioThis ratio shows the company’s ability to pay off all its debts. The higher of this ratio, the company’s ability to pay its liabilities is good, and it is also of course will be a measure for the company to acquire additional new loans from creditors (Hery, 2016).
.

Profitability RatioThe ratio used to measure a company’s ability to generate profits from itrs normal business activities. This ratio is also known as the profitability ratio.

Return on EquityThe ratio shows how much the equity contribution in creating net income. The higher of this ratio, the higher of net profit generated from each Rupiah of funds embedded in equity (Hery, 2016).
.
Reeturn on AssetThis ratio shows how much the contribution of assets in creating net income. The higher of this ratio, it means better for the company in managing its assets to earn net profit.
.
Gross Profit MarginThe ratio that measures the efficiency of price control and the cost of production, indicates the company’s liability to produce efficiently (Sawir, 2009).
.
Operating Profit MarginThe ratio used to measure the percentage of operating profit to net sales. The higher this ratio, the higher of operating profit generated from net sales. That is, the greater this ratio is better (Hery, 2016).
.
Net Profit MarginThe ratio used to measure the percentage of net profit on net sales. The higher net profit margin means higher net income resulting from net sales (Hery, 2016).
.
